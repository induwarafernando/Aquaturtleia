HIKKADUWA TURTLE HATCHERY

WELCOME TO HIKKADUWA TURTLE HATCHERY

We are thrilled to have you here, joining us on a journey to discover and protect the incredible sea turtles of Hikkaduwa. Our hatchery is dedicated to conserving these magnificent creatures, ensuring their survival for generations to come.
Our efforts, we aim to raise awareness about their conservation and provide a safe environment for their eggs to hatch and hatchlings to thrive.
Join us on an unforgettable journey as you immerse yourself in the wonder of nature. Experience the joy of releasing baby turtles into the ocean, knowing that you have contributed to their survival.So, whether you are an avid nature enthusiast, a curious traveler, or someone looking to make a positive impact, we invite you to explore the wonders of the Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery through our website.

VISIT US

The Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery is nestled in the breathtaking coastal town of Hikkaduwa, located on the southwestern coast of Sri Lanka. This idyllic location is renowned for its pristine beaches, crystal-clear waters, and vibrant marine life, making it the perfect setting for a turtle hatchery.
Surrounded by palm-fringed shores and swaying coconut trees, the hatchery is a true haven for nature enthusiasts and beach lovers alike. The tranquil atmosphere, coupled with the beauty of the ocean, creates a sense of serenity that is simply unmatched.
The turquoise waters of Hikkaduwa offer a stunning backdrop for the hatchery's conservation efforts. As you stroll along the sandy beaches, you can witness the enchanting sight of sea turtles gracefully swimming in their natural habitat. These ancient mariners have made Hikkaduwa their home, drawing visitors from all over the world who come to marvel at their majesty.
The location itself showcases the delicate balance between nature and human intervention. The hatchery is strategically positioned to protect sea turtle nests from potential dangers, ensuring a safe environment for their eggs to incubate. By carefully monitoring and safeguarding the nesting sites, the hatchery plays a crucial role in the survival of these endangered species.
